# cultural_memetics.md
## Human Systems Architecture — Module 5

> "Culture is not values on a poster. Culture is the set of behavioral memes that replicate, compete, and dominate within a human system. The strongest memes are not the best. They are the most contagious."

---

## 1. Definition

**Cultural Memetics** = the study of how ideas, behaviors, norms, and narratives replicate, mutate, compete, and dominate within human systems through attention capture, emotional resonance, and political utility.

Culture is not designed. Culture is **selected**.

---

## 2. Core Architecture

### 2.1. Meme Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Mechanism | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Attention Capture** | Novelty, shock, urgency, mystery | "Breaking news" culture |
| **Emotional Resonance** | Fear, hope, outrage, belonging | Us-vs-them narratives |
| **Political Utility** | Advances someone's power | "That's not how we do things here" |
| **Cognitive Ease** | Simple, repeatable, memorable | Slogans, acronyms, buzzwords |
| **Social Proof** | Others are doing it | Bandwagon, conformity |

### 2.2. Meme Lifecycle

```
Emergence → Replication → Competition → Dominance →
Institutionalization → Resistance → Decline / Mutation
```

### 2.3. Culture vs. Declared Values

| Declared Value | Actual Meme | Result |
|---------------|-------------|--------|
| "Innovation" | "Don't rock the boat" | Innovation theater |
| "Transparency" | "Information is power" | Secrecy |
| "Collaboration" | "Protect your turf" | Silos |
| "Customer first" | "Make the numbers" | Short-termism |

## 3. OS Layer Mapping

- **L2 Emotion:** Memes spread through emotional contagion.
- **L3 Cognition:** Mental models are meme containers.
- **L4 Meaning:** Identity is built from dominant memes — "who we are."
- **L5 Action:** Behavior is the observable expression of memes.
- **L6 Awareness:** Meta-awareness of cultural memes — "what story is running me?"

---

## 4. Key Insight

> "You cannot change culture by changing values. You change culture by changing which memes get rewarded, which get punished, and which get ignored. Culture is the behavioral residue of incentive architecture."

---

## 5. Failure Modes

| Failure | Mechanism | Result |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Toxic Meme Dominance** | Negativity, blame, cynicism spread fastest | Culture of fear |
| **Narrative Drift** | Memes mutate away from original intent | Values become meaningless |
| **Memetic Capture** | External memes hijack internal culture | Identity confusion |
| **Resistance Suppression** | Dissenting memes punished | Groupthink, blindness |
| **Cultural Inertia** | Old memes resist new ones | Inability to adapt |

---

## 6. Diagnostic Questions

### For Individuals:
1. What memes am I carrying that I did not choose?
2. What is the most repeated phrase in my environment? What does it actually mean?
3. Who decides what is "normal" here?
4. What meme would get me fired if I challenged it?

### For Systems:
1. What behaviors get rewarded even when they contradict stated values?
2. What stories are told about "success" and "failure"?
3. How do new ideas spread? Or do they die?
4. What is the half-life of a cultural initiative here?

## 8. Protocol — Meme Engineering

### Step 1: Map Dominant Memes
List the 5 most powerful memes. What do they actually produce?

### Step 2: Map Desired Memes
What behaviors do you actually want?

### Step 3: Design Selection Pressure
Reward desired memes. Punish toxic ones. Ignore irrelevant ones.

### Step 4: Create Carrier Behaviors
Make desired memes simple, repeatable, emotionally resonant.

### Step 5: Monitor Mutation
Culture drifts. Audit memes quarterly.
