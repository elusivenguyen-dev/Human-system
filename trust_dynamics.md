# trust_dynamics.md
## Human Systems Architecture — Module 2

> "Trust is not a moral virtue. It is a coordination compression mechanism. Where trust is high, transaction costs drop. Where trust is low, everything slows down, fragments, and dies."

---

## 1. Definition

**Trust Dynamics** = the structural patterns of reliability, predictability, and vulnerability exchange that determine how humans coordinate action, share information, and allocate risk within a system.

Trust is not "liking someone." It is "predicting that they will behave in a way that does not harm me."

---

## 2. Core Architecture

### 2.1. Trust Equation

```
Trust = Competence × Predictability × Integrity × Shared Reality
```

| Component | Definition | Breakdown |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Competence** | Can they do what they say? | Skill, track record, capability |
| **Predictability** | Do they behave consistently? | Reliability, pattern stability |
| **Integrity** | Do they act according to stated values? | Alignment between words and actions |
| **Shared Reality** | Do we agree on what is true? | Common ground, mutual understanding |

**Critical:** If any component = 0, Trust = 0.

### 2.2. Trust Types

| Type | Basis | Use Case |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Calculus-based** | Cost-benefit analysis | Business transactions, contracts |
| **Knowledge-based** | Repeated interaction history | Long-term partnerships |
| **Identification-based** | Shared identity/values | Teams, families, movements |
| **Swift trust** | Immediate role-based assumption | Emergency teams, startups |

### 2.3. Trust Lifecycle

```
Initial Vulnerability → Small Test → Observation → Reciprocity → Deepening → Maintenance
```

## 3. OS Layer Mapping

- **L2 Emotion:** Trust is felt before it is analyzed. Betrayal = emotional trauma.
- **L3 Cognition:** Mental model of "will this person harm me?"
- **L4 Meaning:** Shared identity = identification-based trust.
- **L5 Action:** Execution requires trust to reduce verification overhead.
- **L6 Awareness:** Meta-awareness of trust patterns — "who do I trust and why?"

---

## 4. Key Insight

> "Most organizations do not have a strategy problem. They have a trust collapse problem. When trust breaks, information stops flowing, coordination fragments, and the system reverts to defensive individualism."

---

## 5. Failure Modes

| Failure | Mechanism | Result |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Trust Collapse Cascade** | One betrayal triggers system-wide suspicion | Information freeze, politics |
| **Over-trust** | No verification, blind faith | Exploitation, catastrophic failure |
| **Under-trust** | Constant verification, no delegation | Paralysis, micromanagement |
| **Swift Trust Abuse** | Fake role-based trust | Fraud, infiltration |
| **Identity Trust Polarization** | "Us vs. Them" trust boundaries | Tribalism, exclusion |

---

## 6. Diagnostic Questions

### For Individuals:
1. Who do I trust completely? Why? (Map the 4 components.)
2. Who do I verify constantly? What broke?
3. Have I been betrayed recently? How does that affect my current trust radius?
4. Do I trust myself? (Self-trust = competence + predictability + integrity + reality.)

### For Systems:
1. How long does it take for a new person to be trusted with real decisions?
2. What happens when someone admits a mistake? (Punishment or support?)
3. Is information shared freely or hoarded as power?
4. What is the average "trust repair time" after a conflict?

## 8. Protocol — Trust Repair

### Step 1: Acknowledge Breach
Name what happened. Denial deepens the fracture.

### Step 2: Assess Component Damage
Which of the 4 components broke? (Competence? Predictability? Integrity? Shared Reality?)

### Step 3: Small Reciprocity Loop
Create a low-risk exchange where both parties can observe behavior.

### Step 4: Consistent Pattern Rebuilding
Repeat predictable, competent, integrity-aligned actions over time.

### Step 5: Shared Reality Reconstruction
Re-establish common ground on what is true and what matters.
