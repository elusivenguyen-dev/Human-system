## Daniel Kahneman (1934–)
### Cognitive Biases, Heuristics, Decision Science

**Seminal Works:**
- Kahneman, D., & Tversky, A. (1974). "Judgment under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases." *Science*, 185(4157), 1124–1131. [Foundational paper]
- Tversky, A., & Kahneman, D. (1979). "Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk." *Econometrica*, 47(2), 263–291. [Nobel Prize work]
- Kahneman, D. (2011). *Thinking, Fast and Slow*. Farrar, Straus and Giroux. [System 1 vs. System 2]
- Kahneman, D. (2021). *Noise: A Flaw in Human Judgment*. Little, Brown and Company. [With Olivier Sibony and Cass Sunstein]

**Key Concepts for Human OS:**
- System 1 (Fast): Automatic, intuitive, emotional, heuristic-based
- System 2 (Slow): Deliberate, analytical, effortful, rational
- Availability Heuristic: Judging probability by ease of recall
- Anchoring Effect: Over-reliance on first information
- Loss Aversion: Losses loom larger than gains
- Prospect Theory: Risk attitudes change based on framing

**OS Mapping:** L3 Cognition, L3 Mental Models, L12 Reverse Causality, L6 Biases
